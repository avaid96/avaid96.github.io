Systems Lessons

December 6, 2016

Life lessons from a class in computer systems:

  1. Caches - keep those who matter close
  2. Spatial locality and strides - don’t try to move further ahead than what’s good for you
  3. Buffer overflow - be mindful of all the information you receive
  4. Freeing memory - remember to free your mind of worries that are unneccesary
  5. Virtual memory - fight your limits with the resources that you have
  6. Binary bomb reverse engineering - you just have to ask the right questions
  7. Different machines (little/big endian or 64⁄32 bit) - Different people may interpret the same information differently and you should be mindful of that

And to conclude what I think is the biggest lesson:

  1. When/If you’re working at the higher levels, don’t EVER forget the (processes) people at the lower level who make it possible for you to do what you do

If you want to add to the list or request an edit, please contact me.
