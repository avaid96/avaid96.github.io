Art of war

  1. When you plunder a countryside, let the spoil be divided amongst your men; when you capture new territory, cut it up into allotments for the benefit of the soldiery.
  2. When you surround an army, leave an outlet free. Don’t press a desperate foe too hard.
  3. If your opponent is of choleric temper, seek to irritate him. Pretend to be weak, that he may grow arrogant.
  4. There is no instance of a country having benefited from prolonged warfare.
  5. He will win who
    • knows when to fight and when not to fight
    • knows how to handle both superior and inferior forces.
    • army is animated by the same spirit throughout all its ranks.
    • prepared himself, waits to take the enemy unprepared.
    • has military capacity and is not interfered with by the sovereign.
  6. Thus the good fighter is able to secure himself against defeat, but cannot make certain of defeating the enemy.
  7. Thus it is that in war the victorious strategist only seeks battle after the victory has been won, whereas he who is destined to defeat first fights and afterwards looks for victory.
  8. When in difficult country, do not encamp. In country where high roads intersect, join hands with your allies. Do not linger in dangerously isolated positions.
  9. We shall be unable to turn natural advantage to account unless we make use of local guides.
  10. Too frequent rewards signify that the enemy is at the end of his resources; too many punishments betray a condition of dire distress.
  11. Confront your soldiers with the deed itself; never let them know your design.
  12. Move not unless you see an advantage; use not your troops unless there is something to be gained; fight not unless the position is critical. No ruler should put troops into the field merely to gratify his own spleen; no general should fight a battle simply out of pique.
  13. But a kingdom that has once been destroyed can never come again into being; nor can the dead ever be brought back to life.
  14. In a war, the greatest victory is one that requires no battle.

Ray Dalio

  1. Decide what to do and have the courage to do it
  2. Success is not a matter attaining one’s goals, it is the struggle of evolution towards attaining them
  3. Pain + reflection = progress and meditation is invaluable
  4. 1) know your goals and run after them 2) encounter problems in the way of getting to goals 3) diagnoze these problems to identify their root causes 4) design plan to eliminate them 5) execute those designs
  5. First identify event type, then identify its behavior and outcomes, then tackle it
  6. To get the most out of life, you have to take more risk. Have to balance risk and reward
  7. Our ego and blind spot barriers are the biggest ones we need to deal with. Be radically open minded
  8. We like to believe our own opinions because we like to be right more than we like knowing what’s true
  9. Nobody alone can fully appreciate reality
  10. Replace the joy of being proven right with the joy of knowing what’s true. Find the most thoughtful people who disagree with you. Point out the things you’d individually miss
  11. See beyond yourself and want others to be successful as well
